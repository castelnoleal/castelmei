Transaction Fees ($1.50)

Why is there a $1.50 transaction fee if it is just an Email/Online payment?

I get why you’re wondering about that $1.50 fee, it feels a little odd since it’s “just” an online or email payment, right? The thing is, those payments aren’t as simple as they look. They’re actually processed through a secure, encrypted link or invoice that moves money from your Dedicated Savings Account over to your creditor. And because it’s treated like a billing/service payment, the bank charges a small fee for handling it.
Your creditor agreed to accept payments this way, so that’s the setup we’re working with. The good news is, the fee comes straight out of your DSA, so you don’t have to do anything extra, just keep making your deposits and everything stays on track.

STIP/Stipulations Documents

STIP/Stipulations Document

What's the STIP for? Why do I have to sign it?

That's a good question! It’s basically a legal document which serves as a written confirmation that shows you agree to the settlement terms and what happens if payments stop. Signing it locks in the agreement so your creditor can’t take further legal action as long as you keep up with the scheduled payments until everything’s settled.

Using Debit Card in the Program Deposits

Why can't I use my debit card for my deposits?

I get why you’d ask about using a debit card. Since this is a debt settlement program, deposits have to go through the bank using ACH transfers. ACH is safer, cheaper, and better for recurring payments, while debit cards come with higher fees and more fraud risk. That’s why banks are the only option here.

SSI Proof

Why the need for SSI Proof?

The primary documents to prove you receive Supplemental Security Income (SSI) include an official Benefit Verification Letter (also known as a budget, award, or proof of income letter) from the Social Security Administration (SSA), a recent bank statement showing deposits, or a 1099/1042S tax form. These can be obtained instantly via online Social Security account.

Key Documents for Proving SSI Income:

Benefit Verification Letter (Most Common): This letter serves as official proof of your benefit amount, often called a "budget letter" or "proof of income letter".

Bank Statements: Statements from your bank showing direct deposits from the Social Security Administration. (Must be a PDF file)

Tax Forms (1099/1042S): The annual tax form sent by the SSA reporting your Social Security income.

Award Letter: The original letter sent when you were first approved for benefits, confirming your monthly payment amount.

Representative Payee Documents: If someone else manages your benefits, they can provide documentation confirming your status.

Script - How the NDR Works

How does your program works? What do I need to expect?

Well, National Debt Relief (NDR) is a debt settlement and negotiation program that settles debts by negotiating with your creditors to settle your unsecured debt for LESS than what you actually owe them.

The difference is, instead of you paying your creditors directly, you make these monthly deposits into a what we call dedicated savings account or DSA from which WE will pay your creditors in your behalf.

Upon enrolling to the program, we will require clients to NOT PAY their outstanding debts for 4-6 months to build DELINQUENCY – we use this as leverage to negotiate with the creditors in order to lower down your debts. We usually can drop overall debts to 25-55% lower than your normal debt. We have to take note, however, that this is just the range and is not a guarantee, depends on the creditor.

Also, we require you upon enrollment to deposit at least 20-40% of your debt per creditor as balance to the DSA – this is the only time we will start negotiating with the creditors.

Once enough funds accumulate, NDR negotiates settlements for each debt; you then approve these deals, and the funds inside the Dedicated Savings Account are used to pay the creditors at the reduced rate. You only pay NDR a performance-based fee—after a settlement is successfully reached and funded. This will continue for as long as until you fully settle all of your debts.

Once we had paid all of your creditors, we will celebrate as this will be the time you will graduate from the program, and become debt free!

Reminders - NSF Max Days to Resolve


The resolution window for NSF starts from the date of the call, not the date of the missed deposit.
For example, the client has NSF on 12/11/2025 and the client calls on 02/05/2026:

the 30-day window will be on 03/07/2026, not 01/10/2026

the 60-day window will be on 04/06/2026, not 02/09/2026
